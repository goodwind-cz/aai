## Answers to the dispatch's specific-attention points (judged independently)

**(1) Is crediting growth the right long-term design; is the anti-bloat guard
self-limiting?** Partially, and honestly so. `HEADROOM_CAP` bounds the *per-change
window* (headroom ∈ [0, 2048]), NOT the cumulative credit. This genuinely defeats
*silent* absorption — any growth pushing headroom <0 forces a conscious edit
(bump credit + add a ledger line, or shrink), and the validator proved it bites at
+1 over cap and on shrink-below-floor. But it does NOT force an eventual real
re-diet: an operator can ratchet `JUSTIFIED_GROWTH_BYTES` upward indefinitely so
long as they add a ledger line each time. The spec (§Re-baseline 2) explicitly
accepts this ("add a justified ledger line or shrink"). Verdict: self-limiting
against *unaccounted* growth, not against *deliberate* ratcheting. Acceptable for
an L1 threshold true-up; the reckoning is deferred but made *visible* by
construction. Logged as `cannot_verify` (policy call, not a diff defect).

**(2) Does the ledger itemize per-addition bytes, or is 6144 a lump sum with a
decorative comment?** It is a **lump sum with an itemized list of CAUSES, not of
